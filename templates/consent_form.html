  • DESCRIPTION

    We are researchers at IIT Kharagpur doing a two-part research study to
    understand how users of the Google Pay app feel about the data collected by Google from the app. In this study, we are aiming to perform an investigation of users’ perceptions regarding Google Pay software data collection practices and their privacy norms regarding the same.

     

  • NOTE: Google Pay DOES NOT COLLECT your personally identifiable information in this study from your Google account. YOU ONLY NEED TO SHARE your data collected and stored by Google Pay software in the course of this study to help us understand your perceptions about that particular stored data. Specifically, we DO NOT COLLECT information such as your name, address, emails, email address, date of birth, phone number, images, videos, contacts, location data, ZIP code/postcode, links to social media accounts or posts. We fully abide by Prolific’s rules of not collecting personally identifiable information. This study has been approved by the institute Institute Ethics Committee (IRB).

  • STUDY TASKS:

    In Part 1 of the study, you will first have to participate in a survey. In this survey, you will be required to answer general questions about your usage of Google Pay. You will then be required to install our web browser extension (we will give the installation instructions after you complete the survey). In the last step of Part 1 of the study, you will be asked to share the content of ONLY “Group Expenses”, “Money sends and requests”, “My Activity”, “Rewards Earned”, “Saved items including loyalty gift cards” folders under “Google Pay” folder stored in your Google Account which contains data collected by your Google Pay. You will have to log into your primary Google account (e.g., via https://accounts.google.com/) using the browser. Then our browser extension will assist you in automatically selecting the data required for our study. In this way, the browser extension will ensure that you do not share your entire Google-collected activity data, but just a part of it.

    Finally, you will be required to upload this data to your personal Google Drive and share the link to the data (you will be provided explicit instructions). This will ensure that the data collection is transparent and secure. This data collection will also be the last step of Part 1 of this study. After this step, you will be given the completion token to Part 1 of the study to get your compensation. All of this data that you share with us would only ever be processed by automated means. No researcher will ever manually look at the raw data that you shared and it would be stored on a password-protected server that only the researchers would have access to. Furthermore, we will
    provide you the results of our automated data processing in the course of Part 2 of the study. After the automated processing of your data is completed, we will send you a message on Prolific to return for Part 2 of the study. Part 2 of the study will involve your participation in another final survey. You will be asked a series of questions regarding some of your data collected in Part 1 of the study. These will also be general questions, and the responses will be used to understand user perceptions regarding the use of Google Pay. Each part of the study should take approximately 10 to 15 minutes to complete.

  • RISK AND BENEFITS

    The primary risk to your participation in this online study is unintended leakage of your private data collected by Google Pay and shared with us, which might result in privacy violation and discomfort. We assure you that this data will be kept in a secure password-protected server in an encrypted format, and only the researchers will have access to it. The raw user data will not be accessed by any administrator at any point in the study, as our data processing step is completely automated. Some other risks to your participation in this online study are those associated with basic computer tasks, including boredom, fatigue, mild stress, or breach of confidentiality. The only benefit to you is the learning experience from participating in a research study. The benefit to society is the contribution to scientific knowledge.
